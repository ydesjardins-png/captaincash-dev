## Internal Linking Strategy

### Hub-and-spoke model:
- Every product/SEO page links back to `claim-your-cash.html` (main CTA)
- Every geo page links to its province hub
- Every province hub links to the loans-canada.html pillar
- FAQ answers link to relevant product pages

### Anchor text rules:
- Use descriptive, natural anchor text
- Match anchor to destination page's primary keyword
- Vary anchor text — don't repeat exact same anchor to same URL
- Never use generic anchors: "click here", "learn more" (acceptable only in CTAs)

**Good anchors:**
- "installment loans in Ontario" → `/pages/installment-loans.html`
- "how our loan process works" → `/how-it-works.html`
- "claim your cash today" → `/claim-your-cash.html`
- "loans in Vancouver" → `/loans/british-columbia/vancouver/`

**Avoid:**
- "click here for loans"
- Exact-match keyword stuffing in anchor text

## Geo Page SEO Rules (Critical — SQRG Risk)

Google explicitly penalizes "Scaled Content Abuse" — auto-generated location pages with
zero unique value. Each geo page must pass this test:

**Province hub pages must include:**
1. Province-specific lending regulations (Ontario: max $14/100 for payday; how installment differs)
2. Province-specific eligibility notes (if any variation)
3. Unique introductory paragraph referencing the province specifically
4. Province-specific FAQ answers

**City pages must include:**
1. City-specific opening paragraph (not just "Get a loan in [City]!")
2. Reference to the province the city is in + relevant regulations
3. Unique meta title and description
4. FAQ with at least 1 city-specific question

**What makes a geo page acceptable vs. spam:**
- Acceptable: "Ontario borrowers benefit from provincial installment loan regulations that cap payday loan fees at $14 per $100 — but Captain Cash's installment model is different from a payday loan..."
- Spam: "Get a loan in Toronto! Toronto is a great city. Toronto people need money too!"

## YMYL SEO Content Safety Rules

Before publishing any SEO content:

- [ ] All financial figures are accurate (APR 23%, loan amounts $500–$1,000)
- [ ] No prohibited claims ("guaranteed", "instant", "best rates in Canada")
- [ ] Province availability is accurate for that page
- [ ] Approval is described as subject to eligibility — never guaranteed
- [ ] Cost of borrowing is mentioned with real numbers
- [ ] Responsible borrowing reminder is included
- [ ] E-E-A-T signals are present (company history, licensing, address, phone)
- [ ] Content is genuinely useful — not thin/filler to hit a keyword
