## The Canadian Borrower

### Who is applying for short-term online loans in Canada?

**Demographics:**
- Employed Canadians, 18–55, all income levels
- Income range: $1,200–$3,500/month net (the minimum floor across lenders)
- Often living paycheque to paycheque despite being employed
- May have past credit issues or no credit history
- Gig workers, part-time workers, people between jobs (EI/CPP)
- Geographic spread: urban and small-town Canada

**Their emotional state when searching:**
- Anxious / stressed (unexpected bill, emergency)
- Embarrassed (don't want to ask friends/family)
- Skeptical (worried about scams, hidden fees)
- Time-pressured (needs money today or tomorrow)
- Have often already been rejected by their bank

**Their primary objections:**
1. "Is this a scam?"
2. "Will this ruin my credit?"
3. "What's the real cost? Are there hidden fees?"
4. "Am I even going to qualify?"
5. "How long will it take?"

**What they want to feel by the end of the page:**
- Relieved (this is legitimate)
- Confident (I can afford the payments)
- Hopeful (I'm going to be okay)
- Respected (not judged for needing help)

## Page-by-Page Copywriting Guidelines

### All Pages — Universal Rules:
1. Lead with the outcome, not the process ("Cash in your account today" not "Apply for a loan")
2. Address the #1 objection for that page early
3. Never bury the cost — show it plainly; transparency builds more trust than hiding it
4. Use "you" language throughout — this is about the customer, not Captain Cash
5. Avoid corporate jargon: "financial solutions", "lending products", "facilitate"
6. Keep sentences short. This audience is stressed. They skim.
7. Proof before claims: "12,000+ Canadians served" before "trusted lender"
8. Frame repayment in per-payment amounts, not total cost (easier to digest)

### Homepage (done — index.html):
- ✓ Hero with loan calculator
- ✓ Trust signals up front
- ✓ 3-step process

### Claim Your Cash (apply page):
- Primary emotion: hope + reassurance
- Lead: Remove fear of rejection early
- Emphasize: No credit check, fast, judgment-free
- Show eligibility criteria clearly (so people self-qualify before applying)
- CTA: "Check if I qualify" (softer than "Apply now" — reduces commitment anxiety)

### How It Works:
- Primary emotion: confidence + simplicity
- 3-step process with time estimates ("Takes 5 minutes", "Answer within same day")
- Address speed at every step
- Show what happens AFTER approval (e-Transfer details, payment schedule)
- Include: "What happens if I'm not approved?" (reduces fear of trying)

### Support / FAQ:
- Organize by the questions people are MOST anxious about (not alphabetically)
- Top questions: Am I eligible? How much does it cost? How fast? Will it affect my credit?
- Plain language — no financial jargon
- Show phone/email prominently — accessibility = trustworthiness

### Renew Your Loan:
- Audience: existing customers (warm, already trust Captain Cash)
- Tone: appreciative, efficient, reward loyalty
- Lead with: "Good standing? You may qualify immediately."
- Emphasize: faster process for returning customers

### Online Payment:
- Audience: existing borrowers managing their account
- Tone: helpful, functional, non-judgmental
- Include: What to do if you need to defer a payment (proactive, reduces anxiety)
- Step-by-step e-Transfer instructions

### About:
- Emphasize: Founded 2015, Canadian company, Halifax HQ, 50-person team
- Human story: why Captain Cash was created (bank rejection alternative)
- Trust signals: CLA membership, provincial licensing, encryption
- Avoid: corporate mission statement language

### Contact:
- Show multiple contact methods (phone, email, hours)
- Address: 1701 Hollis Street W. Suite 800, Halifax, NS B3J 2T9
- Phone: 1-888-226-1026
- Emphasize weekend/holiday availability if true

### Testimonials:
- Use REAL testimonials (pull from Trustpilot — even the 2.3 overall has positive reviews)
- Filter for: speed, ease, non-judgment, helped in emergency
- Show customer name + province (location specificity = credibility)
- Balance: don't cherry-pick only 5-star language; authentic mixed sentiment converts better

### SEO Product Pages:
Each page should be 700–1,200 words, contain:
- Province-specific eligibility notes if relevant
- How Captain Cash differs from the "typical" version of that loan type
- FAQ section (3–5 Q&As)
- Clear CTA linking to claim-your-cash

### Geo Pages (Province/City):
- Open with city/province reference (localization = trust)
- Reference local financial pressures where appropriate
- Include: "Available in [Province]" eligibility note
- Avoid: fake local flavour (don't mention specific local attractions unless genuinely relevant)
- Template must avoid duplicate content — use dynamic elements or parameterized sections

## Trust Building Strategy

Captain Cash has a 2.3/5 Trustpilot score. This is a real challenge.
Do not ignore it — build trust through ACTIONS on the site, not empty claims.

### Trust signals that WORK in this market:
1. **Specific numbers** — "12,000+ Canadians served", "since 2015", "50-person team"
2. **Transparent pricing** — show the calculator, show the full repayment amount
3. **Real testimonials** — with name and city
4. **Provincial licensing** — state which provinces Captain Cash is licensed in
5. **CLA membership badge** (Canadian Lenders Association)
6. **Security signals** — SSL, encrypted, bank-level IBV
7. **No-obligation framing** — "free to apply, no commitment to accept"
8. **Direct lender framing** — "we are not a broker; your data stays with us"
9. **Physical address** — showing Halifax HQ builds legitimacy
10. **Phone number prominent** — reachable humans = not a scam

### Trust signals to AVOID (overused, sounds fake):
- "Canada's #1 lender" (unverifiable)
- "Instant approval guaranteed" (misleading)
- "We're the best" (empty)
- Star ratings without a source
- Fake customer photos

## Regulatory & Compliance Notes

- Always include borrowing responsibly disclaimer
- APR must be disclosed: 23%
- Surety fee ($225) and NSF fee ($45) must be mentioned in terms
- Deferral fee: $25 (requires 48 hours notice)
- Do NOT claim "no impact on credit score" unless confirmed accurate
- Do NOT claim "guaranteed approval" — use "high approval rate" or "92% same-day approval"
- Payday loan regulations vary by province — do not overpromise on province pages

## Tone of Voice

**Do:**
- Warm, direct, plain English
- Empathetic without being patronizing
- Confident without being pushy
- Specific (numbers, times, amounts)
- Action-oriented CTAs

**Don't:**
- Corporate speak
- Excessive exclamation marks
- Fear-based urgency ("Don't wait — apply NOW!!!")
- Shame language (never reference "desperation", "broke", "struggling")
- Overly casual (this is still a financial service)

**Voice examples:**
✓ "Apply in 5 minutes. Get your answer today."
✓ "No credit check. No judgment. Just fast cash when you need it."
✓ "Payments that fit your schedule — weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly."
✗ "Get INSTANT cash NOW! Don't miss out!"
✗ "Even if you're broke, we can help you!"
✗ "Our superior financial solutions facilitate your borrowing needs."
