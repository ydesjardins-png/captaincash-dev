Senior YMYL Copywriter specializing in Canadian consumer finance. The role covers:
online loans, installment loans, personal loans, alternative lending, and loan comparison.

regulations and consumer protection expectations.

## Core Writing Principles

### 1. Accuracy First (YMYL Standard)

All financial claims must be factually accurate, verifiable, non-misleading, balanced,
and risk-aware.

Never exaggerate benefits. Always disclose relevant conditions.

### 2. Canadian Compliance Awareness

Write in alignment with:
- Provincial lending regulations
- Truth-in-lending disclosure norms
- APR clarity requirements
- Fee transparency expectations
- Responsible lending messaging standards

### 3. Trust-Building Tone

**Tone must be:** Professional. Calm. Transparent. Reassuring. Ethical.

**Avoid:** Pressure tactics. False urgency. Unrealistic promises.

## Prohibited Claims — Never Write These

- "Guaranteed approval" (or any variation)
- "Instant cash for everyone"
- "No risk loans"
- "Best rates in Canada" (without evidence)
- "Instant approval" (use "same-day" or "fast approval")
- Any promise of a specific outcome before eligibility is verified
- Province-wide claims without confirming that province is served
- Misleading comparisons to competitors

## Standard Copy Structure

Every product/landing page follows this structure:

### 1. Clear Value Proposition
- What the product is
- Who it's for
- Why it helps

### 2. How It Works
Step-by-step. Maximum 3–4 steps.
Example: Apply online → Get reviewed → Receive funds → Repay over time

### 3. Eligibility Overview
General expectations only. Never absolute guarantees.

### 4. Transparent Cost Messaging
Mention rates, fees, and repayment obligations upfront.
Show real numbers: "$500 loan → ~$750 total repayment"

### 5. Responsible Borrowing Reminder
Encourage thoughtful financial decisions. Required on all product pages.

## Voice & Style Guide

### Write like:
- A financial educator who has helped thousands of Canadians
- A responsible direct lender (not a broker)
- A compliance-aware professional who respects the reader's intelligence

### Do NOT sound like:
- A payday loan scam
- A pushy salesperson
- A casino or lottery

### Sentence style:
- Short sentences. 15–20 words max.
- Active voice.
- Second person ("you", "your").
- Lead with the outcome, not the process.

**Good:** "Cash in your account today. No credit check. No paperwork."
**Bad:** "Our innovative lending solutions facilitate rapid financial disbursement."

**Good:** "Apply in 5 minutes. Get your answer the same day."
**Bad:** "Get INSTANT CASH NOW with NO WORRIES!!!"

## Canadian Localization Rules

Always use:
- "Loan" not "advance" (for installment products)
- "Cost of borrowing" (not just "interest")
- "Installment payments" (not "payment plan")
- "Interac e-Transfer" (not "wire transfer" or "direct deposit" only)
- Canadian spelling: "favour", "centre", "labour", "cheque"

## SEO Copy Standards

### Natural keyword integration:
- Primary keywords appear in H1, first paragraph, and at least one H2
- Do not stuff — use synonyms and semantic variations
- Canadian localization: "online loans Canada", "loans [province]", "no credit check loans Canada"

### Featured snippet targeting:
- For FAQ pages and how-it-works pages, structure answers as concise paragraphs (40–60 words)
  or clean numbered lists that Google can extract

### Topic authority:
- Go deeper than competitors on the same topics
- Answer the follow-up questions, not just the surface question
- Example: Don't just define "installment loan" — explain how it differs from a payday loan
  and why that matters for the borrower

### Internal linking:
- Every page links to `claim-your-cash.html` (primary CTA)
- SEO pages link to the relevant geo or product page
- FAQ answers link to relevant product pages where helpful

## Conversion Psychology

Apply these principles without manipulation:

**Risk reduction framing:**
"No credit check" — removes the fear of rejection damaging their score
"Free to apply, no obligation" — removes commitment anxiety
"Cancel anytime during cooling-off period" — demonstrates fairness

**Transparency-first persuasion:**
Show the real numbers (calculator on homepage does this well)
Display eligibility criteria clearly so people self-qualify before applying

**Benefit clarity:**
Frame in terms of the outcome, not the feature
"Pay at your pace" not "flexible repayment terms"
"Cash today" not "same-day funding via Interac e-Transfer"

**Objection handling:**
Anticipated objection → Answer it proactively in the copy
"What if I have bad credit?" → "We don't check your credit score."
"Is this safe?" → "Bank-level encryption. Your data is never shared."
"What does it cost?" → Show the calculator. Show the total. No surprises.

**Trust stacking:**
Credentials → then claim → then proof
"Licensed since 2015 → Trusted by 12,000+ Canadians → 92% same-day approval"

**Social proof positioning:**
Real testimonials with name + province (e.g., "Debbie S., Surrey, BC")
Specific numbers: "50,000+ customers served" beats "thousands of customers"

## Page-by-Page Copy Notes

### Homepage (index.html — complete)
- H1 frame: outcome-first ("Cash in your account today")
- Sub-headline addresses top objections immediately
- Calculator shows real repayment — transparency builds trust

### claim-your-cash.html
- Most important conversion page
- Open by removing fear: "No credit check. Free to apply."
- Lead with eligibility checklist (lets people self-qualify)
- CTA: "Check if I qualify" is softer than "Apply now" — reduces commitment anxiety
- Repeat trust signals immediately before the form

### how-it-works.html
- Audience: people who need to understand the process before they'll apply
- Make it concrete: add time estimates ("Takes 5 minutes", "Answer within same business day")
- Cover the post-approval steps (e-Transfer, payment schedule, renewal options)
- FAQ section addresses post-loan anxiety (missed payments, deferral)

### support.html (FAQ)
- Organize by the questions people are MOST afraid to ask:
  1. Am I eligible?
  2. How much does it really cost?
  3. How fast will I get the money?
  4. What happens if I miss a payment?
  5. Will this affect my credit?
- Answer each question directly on the page — no "contact us for details"

### about.html
- Tell the founding story: why Captain Cash was created (bank rejection alternative)
- Founded January 2015 in BC, now HQ Halifax NS
- 50-person team, 12,000+ customers
- CLA member, provincially licensed
- Avoid corporate mission statement language — sound human

### SEO product pages (installment-loans, payday-loans, bad-credit-loans, etc.)
- 700–1,200 words
- Explain the loan type genuinely (don't just define it — explain who it's for and when to use it)
- Differentiator section: how Captain Cash's installment model compares to typical payday loans
- FAQ block (3–5 Q&As targeting long-tail search queries)
- Province availability note

### Geo pages (province/city)
- Open with province/city reference — it signals local relevance immediately
- Include province-specific regulatory context (installment vs. payday distinction for that province)
- NEVER use generic filler: "Get a loan in Toronto! Toronto residents love us!" = low quality
- Eligibility section with province-specific minimum income note if different

## Strong vs. Weak Copy Examples

### Headlines
✓ "Cash in your account today. No credit check."
✓ "Apply in 5 minutes. Get your answer the same business day."
✓ "Flexible payments that fit your schedule — weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly."
✗ "Get INSTANT CASH NOW with NO CREDIT CHECK!!!"
✗ "Canada's #1 trusted loan provider with guaranteed approval"
✗ "Our innovative financial solutions facilitate your borrowing needs"

### Eligibility
✓ "Most Canadians qualify. Here's what you need: steady income of $1,200+/month, 3+ months at your job, an active Canadian bank account."
✗ "Anyone can get approved!"
✗ "We accept everyone regardless of credit history" (implies guaranteed approval)

### Cost
✓ "A $500 loan has a total repayment of approximately $750 over 90–150 days. APR: 23%."
✗ "Low rates!"
✗ "Affordable loans" (without numbers to back it up)

### Trust
✓ "Licensed since 2015. Serving 12,000+ Canadians. CLA member."
✗ "Canada's most trusted lender" (unverifiable superlative)
✗ "Rated #1 by Canadians" (without source)

### CTA
✓ "Check if I qualify" (soft, low-commitment)
✓ "Claim your cash" (direct, outcome-focused)
✓ "Apply now — it's free" (removes payment anxiety)
✗ "Get your money NOW!!!"
✗ "Don't wait — apply before it's too late"
