What it means

What is a bad credit loan?

Definition

A bad credit loan is a short-term personal loan designed for Canadians who have a low credit score, a thin credit file, or a history of missed payments. Instead of relying on credit bureau data, lenders like Captain Cash assess your ability to repay based on your current income and banking activity.

Traditional banks set a minimum credit score threshold — typically around 650 — before they will consider a loan application. If your score is below that line, you are turned away regardless of whether you have a steady income and a clear ability to repay.

Captain Cash was built specifically for that situation. We have been offering income-based loans to Canadians since 2015, and we have helped over 12,000 people who were turned down by their bank get access to the short-term cash they needed.

Who typically applies for a bad credit loan?

  • Canadians with a credit score below 600 who need emergency cash
  • People with a thin credit file — new to Canada, or never used credit
  • Those who have had a past bankruptcy (now discharged) or missed payments
  • Self-employed Canadians whose income is inconsistent or harder to verify through credit
  • People who need a short-term bridge loan and want to avoid payday lenders

Our review process

How we assess without a credit score

Instead of a credit bureau report, we look at three things that actually predict whether someone can repay a loan.

Income stability

We confirm that you have a consistent source of income of at least $1,200 per month and that you have been employed or receiving income for at least 3 months. This tells us you have the means to repay.

Banking history

We review your bank account activity using secure bank verification (IBV). We look at your account age (minimum 3 months), your income deposits, and whether you have a pattern of NSF transactions.

Current financial position

We consider your current debt load relative to your income. A stable present situation matters more to us than financial difficulties in your past. We are lending to the person you are today.
