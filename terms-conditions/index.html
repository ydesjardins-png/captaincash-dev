Section 1

Eligibility requirements

To apply for a Captain Cash installment loan, you must meet all of the following criteria at the time of application:

  • Be a Canadian resident aged 18 years or older
  • Reside in a province or territory where Captain Cash is licensed: Ontario, British Columbia, Alberta, Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, Prince Edward Island, Newfoundland and Labrador, Nunavut, or the Northwest Territories
  • Have a minimum monthly net income of $1,200, received by direct deposit into a Canadian bank account
  • Have been continuously employed or receiving consistent income for a minimum of 3 months
  • Hold an active Canadian bank account that has been open for at least 3 months and into which your income is deposited by direct deposit
  • Have few or no non-sufficient fund (NSF) transactions on your bank account
  • Not be currently bankrupt, insolvent, or subject to an active consumer proposal

Note: Captain Cash is not currently licensed to lend in Quebec, Manitoba, or Saskatchewan. Applications from residents of these provinces cannot be processed. Meeting the minimum eligibility criteria does not guarantee approval — all applications are subject to our internal assessment process.

Section 2

Loan terms

Captain Cash offers personal installment loans on the following terms:

  • Loan amounts: $500 to $1,000 CAD
  • Repayment period: 90 to 150 days from the date of disbursement
  • Annual Percentage Rate (APR): 23% on all loans
  • Payment frequency: Weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly — aligned to your pay cycle
  • Disbursement method: Interac e-Transfer to your registered Canadian bank account
  • Transaction fee: $0.50 per transaction

Representative example

A $500 loan at APR 23% over 90–150 days results in a total repayment of approximately $750, inclusive of all interest and fees. Actual repayment amounts depend on your approved term and payment schedule, which are disclosed in full in your loan agreement before you accept.

Important: You will receive a complete loan agreement showing every scheduled payment, the total cost of borrowing, and the APR before you are required to sign. You are under no obligation to accept the loan after reviewing the agreement.

Section 3

Repayment

Repayment is made via pre-authorized debit (PAD) from the Canadian bank account registered in your application. Payments are scheduled on your pay dates — weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly — as selected during the application process.

Your complete repayment schedule is provided in your loan agreement. Each payment covers a portion of principal plus accrued interest. All payments are applied first to outstanding fees, then to interest, then to principal.

Changing your repayment date

If your pay date changes, contact us at least 5 business days in advance at 1-888-226-1026 or support@captaincash.ca and we will adjust your schedule accordingly, subject to the terms of your loan agreement.

Missed payments

If a scheduled payment cannot be processed due to insufficient funds, a $45 NSF fee will be applied to your account and the missed payment will be rescheduled to the end of your contract term. Missed payments do not affect your credit score with Captain Cash as we do not report to credit bureaus.

If you anticipate difficulty making a scheduled payment, contact us at least 48 hours before the payment date to arrange a deferral for a $25 fee.

Section 4

Fees and charges

All fees applicable to your loan are disclosed in your loan agreement before you sign. The following fees may apply:

  • Application fee: None — applying is free and there is no obligation to accept
  • NSF fee: $45 per returned payment (insufficient funds)
  • Deferral fee: $25 per deferred payment (requires 48 hours advance notice)
  • Prepayment penalty: None — you may repay your loan early at any time at no charge
  • Transaction fee: $0.50 per disbursement transaction

No hidden fees: There are no administration fees, processing fees, or any other charges beyond those listed above. The total cost of borrowing — including all fees and interest — is displayed in your loan agreement before you accept.

Section 5

Cancellation rights

In provinces where applicable provincial legislation provides a cooling-off period (including Ontario and British Columbia), you have the right to cancel your loan agreement within 2 business days of signing without penalty or cost.

To exercise your right to cancel, contact us in writing within the applicable cancellation period:

  • By email: support@captaincash.ca
  • By phone: 1-888-226-1026
  • By mail: 1701 Hollis Street W. Suite 800, Halifax, NS B3J 2T9

If you cancel within the cooling-off period, you must return the full loan amount. No interest or fees will be charged for the cancellation period.

Section 6

Early repayment

You may repay the outstanding balance of your loan in full at any time prior to your scheduled final payment date. There is no prepayment penalty.

Early repayment reduces the total interest you pay — you are charged interest only for the days you hold the loan. To make an early repayment or obtain a payoff amount, contact us at 1-888-226-1026 or support@captaincash.ca to stop any scheduled automatic withdrawals before making your final payment.

Section 7

Default and collections

Your loan is considered in default if you fail to make a scheduled payment and have not arranged a deferral. Upon default, the full outstanding balance of your loan, including accrued interest and applicable fees, becomes immediately due and payable.

Our collection approach

Captain Cash uses responsible collection practices in accordance with applicable provincial consumer protection legislation. We will always contact you directly before escalating to any collection process. Our first step is always to attempt to reach a mutually agreed resolution.

If you are experiencing financial difficulty, please contact us as early as possible. We would rather work with you than pursue formal collection. Call us at 1-888-226-1026 during business hours.

Escalation process

If direct contact and internal resolution attempts are unsuccessful, Captain Cash may:

  • Terminate the payment plan and declare the full balance owing
  • Assign the account to a licensed collection agency
  • Pursue legal action to recover the outstanding balance

Captain Cash does not report loan accounts to credit bureaus. However, accounts assigned to collection agencies may be subject to that agency's reporting practices.

Section 8

Renewals

Existing Captain Cash customers in good standing may be eligible to renew their loan after completing their current loan or during a grace period before their new loan's payment cycles begin. Renewal eligibility is assessed at the time of application and is not guaranteed.

Returning customers who have successfully repaid a previous loan may also be eligible to apply using Employment Insurance (EI) as a qualifying income source. This option is not available to first-time applicants.

Section 9

Interac e-Transfer disbursement

Approved loans are funded by Interac e-Transfer to the bank account registered in your application. If your application is approved before 2:00 PM Eastern Time on a business day, funds are sent the same business day. Applications approved after 2:00 PM ET or on weekends or statutory holidays are processed the next business day.

Captain Cash is not responsible for delays caused by your financial institution's processing times. Interac e-Transfer funds are typically deposited within minutes but may take up to several hours depending on your bank.

You must accept the e-Transfer within the timeframe specified by Interac. If the transfer expires before you accept it, contact us to re-initiate the transfer.

Repayments are collected by pre-authorized debit payable to receivables@diamondfinancialone.com. This is our payment processing entity — please ensure this address is recognized by your bank to avoid returned payments.
