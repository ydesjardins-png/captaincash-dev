What is a bad credit loan?

A loan designed for people the banks turned away

A bad credit loan is a personal loan offered to borrowers who have a low credit score, a limited credit history, or past financial difficulties — circumstances that would typically disqualify them from a traditional bank loan.

Most Canadians do not have perfect credit. Missed payments, high credit utilization, a thin file, or a past bankruptcy can all drag a score down — even if your current financial situation is stable. Traditional lenders use that number to make a binary decision. We do not.

Captain Cash is a direct lender, not a broker. We built our assessment model around income and banking activity because those are far better predictors of whether someone can repay a short-term loan. A borrower with a 580 credit score who has held the same job for three years and keeps a consistent account balance is a good candidate. A borrower with a 720 score who has had six jobs in two years may not be.

Our loans are installment loans — not payday loans. You repay in scheduled payments over 90 to 150 days, not in a single lump sum. This makes the repayment manageable and predictable.

How we assess you

What we look at instead of your credit score

A credit score tells a lender how you handled debt in the past. It does not tell them whether you can handle a $500 repayment next month. For short-term lending, income and banking stability are far more relevant signals.

Who qualifies

Eligibility requirements

Bad credit is not a barrier — but you do need to meet these minimum requirements. They exist to protect you as much as us.

You must:

✓Be a Canadian resident, 18 years or older
✓Live in a province we serve (ON, BC, AB, NS, NB, PEI, NL, Nunavut, NWT)
✓Earn at least $1,200 per month, received by direct deposit
✓Have 3+ months of stable employment or consistent income
✓Have an active Canadian bank account open for 3+ months
✓Have few or no NSF (non-sufficient fund) transactions
✓Not be currently bankrupt or in a consumer proposal
Not available in: Quebec, Manitoba, or Saskatchewan. Captain Cash is not licensed to lend in these provinces.

Income sources we accept:

✓Employment income (full-time or part-time)
✓Self-employment income
✓Employment Insurance (EI) — after a successful first loan repayment

Income sources we do not accept:

✕Social assistance or welfare
✕Student loans
✕Child tax benefit
✕CPP or private pension
✕Disability income

Approval is subject to eligibility verification. Meeting minimum criteria does not guarantee approval.
