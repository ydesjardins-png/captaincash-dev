The definition

What is an installment loan in Canada?

An installment loan is a type of loan where you borrow a fixed amount and repay it in scheduled payments over a set period — typically 90 to 150 days — rather than in a single lump sum at your next payday.

Each payment covers a portion of the principal plus interest, calculated at the agreed APR. By the end of the term, the loan is fully repaid. The payment schedule is set before you sign — you know exactly what you owe and when.

Captain Cash installment loans are designed for Canadians who need a short-term cash bridge — an unexpected bill, a gap between paycheques, or an urgent expense — and want to repay it gradually rather than all at once.

Unlike payday loans, which demand full repayment on your next payday, installment loans spread the cost over multiple pay cycles. This makes each payment smaller and less likely to leave your account empty.

How repayment works

Your payment schedule, explained

When you are approved, Captain Cash calculates a repayment schedule based on three inputs: your loan amount, your pay frequency, and your next pay date. You choose how often you want to pay — weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly — and your payments align to those dates automatically.

The schedule is shown to you in full before you sign. Every payment amount, every date, and the total cost of the loan are visible before any commitment is made.

If your financial situation changes, you can request a deferral with 48-hour notice for a $25 fee — the deferred payment is added to the end of your contract. You can also repay the full balance early at any time with no penalty.

Missed payment fee: $45 NSF fee if a scheduled payment cannot be processed. If you know a payment will be missed, contact us 48 hours in advance — a $25 deferral fee is far less disruptive than a $45 NSF charge.

Why installment

Why an installment loan works better than a payday loan

Same speed, same accessibility — but a repayment structure that doesn't wipe out your paycheque.

Smaller, manageable payments

Instead of repaying $650+ in one lump sum on your next payday, you repay in equal installments over 3 to 5 months. Each payment is a fraction of your paycheque — not the whole thing.

Aligned to your pay cycle

Your repayment schedule is built around your actual pay dates — weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly. You are never paying on a random date. The money comes out when it is in your account.

Full transparency before you sign

Every payment, every date, the APR, and the total cost of borrowing are shown in your loan agreement before you accept. There are no surprises after approval.
