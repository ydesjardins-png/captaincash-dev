The basics

What is a credit check — and why does it matter?

When you apply for a loan, credit card, or mortgage, the lender typically runs a credit check through Equifax or TransUnion. This pulls your credit report and may record an inquiry. Not all inquiries are equal.

There are two types: soft inquiries and hard inquiries. Most people worried about credit checks are worried about hard inquiries — and for good reason.

Soft inquiry
Used for pre-approvals, background checks, and checking your own score. Does not appear on your credit report and has no effect on your score.
Hard inquiry
Triggered when you formally apply for credit. Lowers your score by 5–10 points and stays on your report for up to 2 years. Multiple hard inquiries in a short window raise red flags.

Captain Cash performs neither. We do not access your credit file at all. There is no inquiry — soft or hard — as part of our assessment process.

Why it matters for you

Shopping for a loan can hurt your score before you get one

Here is a situation many Canadians face: you need a loan urgently. You apply to three or four lenders. Each one runs a hard inquiry. Your score drops 20–30 points in the span of a week. Now you are a higher-risk borrower, which affects your mortgage application, car loan, or even apartment rental.

With Captain Cash, this does not happen. One application. No inquiry. No impact on the score you have been building.

Important: A no credit check loan does not build or repair your credit history either. If rebuilding credit is a goal, you may want to explore secured credit products alongside short-term loans. Captain Cash recommends borrowing only what you need for a specific short-term purpose.
